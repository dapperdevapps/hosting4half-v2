Common questions

questions
worth answering.

The ones we actually get asked, written out the way we'd answer them. If you have a different one, the form above is the fastest place to put it.

How is the audit free?

Hosting providers pay a referral when we migrate a client onto their platform — same model as a mortgage broker. The fee comes out of their margin, not your savings. We only get paid if you actually move, and we only recommend a move when the math is on your side.

What if my current plan is already right-priced?

It happens about 1 in 6 audits. We say so on page one of the report, point out the few small things you could still tighten, and that's the end of it. There's no consolation upsell.

What information do I have to share?

The invoice or a screenshot of your plan page is all we need. No WordPress login, no DNS access, no customer-facing credentials. Plan, traffic estimate, and storage size are enough for the forensic review.

How long does a migration take?

Most are done inside 24 – 48 hours including staging, regression on every page, and DNS propagation. Live site stays untouched until the new build passes our checks. Cutover usually takes 12 minutes.

What about email if I switch hosts?

Email gets handled separately. We'll either leave it on your current MX records, move it to Google Workspace, or set it up with a dedicated mail provider. We never cut email during a migration without a tested fallback path.

Will switching affect SEO?

A clean migration has zero negative SEO impact. We verify redirects, canonicals, sitemaps, and Search Console after every move. Faster infrastructure usually nudges Core Web Vitals up — which Google reads as a positive ranking signal.

I have multiple sites. Can you audit all of them?

Yes — multi-site is where the savings are usually largest. Hosts love bundling four sites onto a managed plan that costs more than one well-tuned VPS. Mention how many sites you run in the notes field and we'll cover them in one report.

What does the migration cost, if I do switch?

A flat $480 for a single WordPress site, $260 for each additional site in the same migration. No retainer, no hidden hours. The first month at the new host usually pays for it.
